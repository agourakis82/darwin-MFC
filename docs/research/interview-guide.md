# Darwin-MFC Interview Guide
## Semi-Structured Interview Protocol for Health Practitioners

---

## Pre-Interview Preparation

### Logistics
- **Duration:** 30-45 minutes
- **Platform:** WhatsApp voice/video (most accessible in LMICs)
- **Recording:** Audio only with explicit consent
- **Transcription:** Whisper AI, followed by manual review

### Before the Call
1. Review participant's survey responses (if available)
2. Note their country, role, and experience level
3. Prepare culturally appropriate greetings
4. Test audio/video connection

### Materials Needed
- Interview guide (this document)
- Consent checklist
- Note-taking template
- Recording device/app

---

## Consent Script (Read Verbatim)

> "Hello, my name is [Interviewer Name] from the Darwin-MFC project. Thank you for agreeing to speak with me today.
>
> Before we begin, I want to explain a few things:
> - This interview is for academic research to improve clinical decision support tools
> - Your participation is completely voluntary
> - You can skip any question or end the interview at any time
> - I would like to record our conversation for accurate transcription
> - The recording will be transcribed, de-identified, and then deleted
> - Your name will not appear in any reports
> - There is no compensation for participation
>
> Do you have any questions before we start?
> Do I have your permission to record this conversation?"

**Consent Checklist:**
- [ ] Purpose explained
- [ ] Voluntary participation confirmed
- [ ] Right to skip/end confirmed
- [ ] Recording consent obtained
- [ ] De-identification explained
- [ ] No compensation confirmed

## Interview Sections

### Opening (5 minutes)

**Ice-breaker:**
> "Before we dive in, could you tell me a little about yourself? Where do you work, and what does a typical day look like for you?"

**Probes:**
- What time do you start your day?
- How many patients do you typically see?
- What types of conditions are most common?

---

### Section 1: Practice Context (10 minutes)

**Main Question 1.1:**
> "Tell me about the resources you currently use when you're uncertain about a clinical decision."

**Probes:**
- Do you use any apps or websites? Which ones?
- How about colleagues or specialists?
- What do you do when internet is not available?
- Are there resources you wish you had but don't?

**Main Question 1.2:**
> "Think about a recent case where you felt uncertain. Can you walk me through how you handled it?"

**Probes:**
- What was the uncertainty about? (diagnosis, treatment, referral)
- How did you resolve it?
- How long did it take to find an answer?
- Were you satisfied with the outcome?

**Main Question 1.3:**
> "What are the biggest frustrations you face in your daily practice?"

**Probes:**
- Time constraints?
- Access to information?
- System/administrative issues?
- Patient volume?

---

### Section 2: Digital Tools (10 minutes)

**Main Question 2.1:**
> "What digital tools do you currently use in your clinical work?"

**Probes:**
- For each tool: What do you like about it? What frustrates you?
- How did you learn about these tools?
- How often do you use them?
- Do you pay for any of them?

**Main Question 2.2:**
> "If you could design the perfect clinical decision support tool, what would it look like?"

**Probes:**
- What features are essential?
- What should it NOT do?
- How should it be organized?
- What about language preference?

**Main Question 2.3:**
> "What prevents you from using digital tools more?"

**Probes:**
- Technical barriers? (internet, devices)
- Time barriers?
- Trust in information?
- Cost?
- Institutional factors?

---

### Section 3: Learning & Community (10 minutes)

**Main Question 3.1:**
> "How do you currently keep up with medical knowledge?"

**Probes:**
- Conferences? CME?
- Reading journals?
- Online courses?
- Peer discussions?
- How much time do you dedicate to learning?

**Main Question 3.2:**
> "Have you ever participated in an online professional community? Tell me about that experience."

**Probes:**
- What worked well?
- What didn't work?
- Would you do it again?
- If never: What would make you consider joining one?

**Main Question 3.3:**
> "What do you think about the idea of sharing anonymized clinical cases with peers for learning?"

**Probes:**
- What concerns would you have?
- What safeguards would you need?
- Would you contribute or just observe?
- What types of cases would you share?

**Main Question 3.4:**
> "If there was a mentorship program connecting you with experienced practitioners, would you be interested?"

**Probes:**
- As a mentor or mentee?
- What topics would you want to discuss?
- How often would you engage?
- What format would work best?

---

### Section 4: Connectivity & Access (5 minutes)

**Main Question 4.1:**
> "Tell me about your internet situation at work."

**Probes:**
- Is it reliable?
- Do you have data limits?
- What happens when it's down?
- How important is offline access for you?

**Main Question 4.2:**
> "What devices do you typically use during clinical work?"

**Probes:**
- Personal or institutional device?
- Smartphone, tablet, computer?
- Screen size concerns?
- Any device limitations?

---

### Closing (5 minutes)

**Wrap-up Question:**
> "Is there anything else about your needs as a healthcare professional that we haven't covered?"

**Future Participation:**
> "We're developing this platform based on feedback like yours. Would you be interested in testing early versions and giving us feedback?"

**Referrals:**
> "Do you know any colleagues who might be interested in participating in this research? We're looking for diverse perspectives from different countries and settings."

**Thank You:**
> "Thank you so much for your time today. Your insights are incredibly valuable for building something that truly helps practitioners like yourself. Do you have any questions for me before we end?"

## Interview Tips

### Cultural Considerations

**Brazil:**
- Warm, relationship-oriented communication
- Allow time for personal connection
- Informal tone is appropriate

**India:**
- May defer to perceived authority
- Encourage honest opinions explicitly
- Be aware of hierarchy sensitivity

**Nigeria:**
- Direct communication style
- Professional tone appreciated
- Acknowledge time constraints

**General:**
- Be patient with connectivity issues
- Reschedule gracefully if needed
- Respect time zones
- Send reminder 24 hours before

### Handling Difficult Situations

**Participant goes off-topic:**
> "That's interesting. Let me make a note of that. Going back to..."

**Participant gives short answers:**
> "Can you tell me more about that?"
> "What was that experience like for you?"

**Participant is emotional about challenges:**
> "It sounds like that's been really difficult. Thank you for sharing that."

**Technical issues:**
> "I'm having some trouble hearing you. Would you mind repeating that?"
> "Let's try switching to audio only."

## Quality Assurance

### Interview Quality Checklist

After each interview, rate:
- [ ] Consent properly obtained
- [ ] All sections covered
- [ ] Sufficient depth achieved
- [ ] Recording quality acceptable
- [ ] Participant comfortable throughout
- [ ] New insights captured

### Red Flags to Note

- Participant mentions dangerous clinical situations
- Signs of burnout or distress
- Institutional barriers that affect patient care
- Specific tool failures with patient impact

*Report significant concerns to research lead.*
